# Where to practise next

These labs are a starting point. When you've worked through them, here's the wider ecosystem of
hands-on security practice — grouped by what it's good for, and mapped to the parts of this series it
extends.

> **Practise responsibly.** Only attack systems you own or are explicitly authorised to test. Everything
> below is either self-hosted, deliberately vulnerable by design, or an authorised practice range. These
> are external projects — we're not affiliated with them; check each one's own terms.

## Guided, beginner-friendly

Structured paths that hold your hand — good right after these labs.

- **[TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com/)** — guided "rooms" from absolute beginner up; browser-based. (Free tier + subscription.)
- **[PortSwigger Web Security Academy](https://portswigger.net/web-security)** — the best free web-security training there is, from the makers of Burp Suite. *Extends: Ethical Hacking (web).*
- **[PentesterLab](https://pentesterlab.com/)** — focused exploitation exercises with clear explanations.
- **[picoCTF](https://picoctf.org/)** — beginner CTF from Carnegie Mellon; great first taste of challenges.

## Vulnerable targets you run yourself

Download-and-hack, in the same self-hosted spirit as these labs.

- **[VulnHub](https://www.vulnhub.com/)** — a huge library of deliberately vulnerable **VM images** to download and attack. *Extends: Ethical Hacking, Lateral Movement.*
- **[Metasploitable 2 / 3](https://github.com/rapid7/metasploitable3)** — the classic vulnerable target VM.
- **[OWASP Juice Shop](https://owasp.org/www-project-juice-shop/)** & **[WebGoat](https://owasp.org/www-project-webgoat/)** — deliberately vulnerable web apps (Juice Shop already appears in our labs). *Extends: Ethical Hacking (web).*
- **[DVWA](https://github.com/digininja/DVWA)** — Damn Vulnerable Web Application (also used in our labs).

## Wargames & CTF

Puzzle-style challenges that build fundamentals.

- **[OverTheWire](https://overthewire.org/wargames/)** — start with **Bandit** (Linux/SSH basics), then Leviathan, Natas (web), Narnia (binaries).
- **[Root-Me](https://www.root-me.org/)** — hundreds of challenges across every category.
- **[Exploit Education](https://exploit.education/)** (Phoenix, Nebula) — binary exploitation, a natural next step after our buffer-overflow lab. *Extends: Ethical Hacking (exploit dev).*
- **[CTFtime](https://ctftime.org/)** — calendar of live CTF competitions and team rankings.

## Blue team & digital forensics

The defensive/analysis counterpart — closest to *Assume Breach* and *Forensics*.

- **[CyberDefenders](https://cyberdefenders.org/)** & **[Blue Team Labs Online](https://blueteamlabs.online/)** — DFIR and detection challenges. *Extends: Digital Forensics, Assume Breach.*
- **Evidence datasets** (real images to analyse in our forensics lab):
  [Digital Corpora](https://digitalcorpora.org/),
  [NIST CFReDS](https://cfreds.nist.gov/),
  [Volatility sample images](https://github.com/volatilityfoundation/volatility/wiki/Memory-Samples). *Extends: Digital Forensics.*

## Reference & knowledge

Not challenges — the material you reach for mid-challenge.

- **[MITRE ATT&CK](https://attack.mitre.org/)** — the adversary tactics/techniques taxonomy (also the backbone of *Incident Zero*).
- **[OWASP Top 10](https://owasp.org/www-project-top-ten/)** — the canonical web-risk list.
- **[HackTricks](https://book.hacktricks.xyz/)**, **[GTFOBins](https://gtfobins.github.io/)**, **[PayloadsAllTheThings](https://github.com/swisskyrepo/PayloadsAllTheThings)** — the working pentester's cheat sheets.

## Managed / online labs

Zero-setup, gamified ranges (mostly freemium).

- **[Hack The Box](https://www.hackthebox.com/)** — pentesting machines + a structured Academy.
- **[TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com/)** — (also listed above) the gentlest on-ramp.
